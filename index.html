Sources & Omega Buckets

A healthy balance of omega‑6 and omega‑3 fatty acids supports heart health and reduces inflammation. Nutrition experts recommend keeping the ratio of omega‑6 to omega‑3 between 1:1 and 4:1【557145189711555†L220-L224】. Modern diets often exceed this range due to high consumption of processed foods and seed oils. Below are examples of foods that fall into each category【775034953345055†L144-L151】:

Omega‑3 Sources

  • Purslane【788540841835043†L208-L211】
  • Mussels & Oysters【548870733932692†L97-L115】
  • Salmon
  • Sardines
  • Mackerel & Herring
  • Flaxseeds & Chia Seeds
  • Walnuts

Omega‑6 Sources

  • Safflower, Sunflower, Corn & Soybean Oils【775034953345055†L144-L147】
  • Sunflower & Pumpkin Seeds
  • Walnuts
  • Processed Condiments (hummus, pesto)【480202975198168†L360-L364】
