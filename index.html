The Weekly Approach

A few things worth calling out for the week:

  • Monday through Thursday front-loads the high EPA+DHA meals (salmon, sardines, mackerel) so you're getting direct anti-inflammatory omega-3 early in the week.
  • Friday's chia oats round it out with ALA-rich plant sources, which is a lighter finish to the week.
  • The Sunday batch cook is the real force multiplier — roasting the sweet potato and steaming the broccoli once means Thursday's bento literally takes 5 minutes to pack.
  • The quinoa batch carries Monday and Wednesday, and the lemon-EVOO-turmeric dressing works across three meals, so you make it once.

Total Sunday prep time is realistically 60–75 minutes including cleanup, and then each night-before task is 5–10 minutes.

Fatty Acid Sourcing Matrix

A breakdown of Omega-3 vs Omega-6 sources based on local accessibility.

Omega-3 • Easy to Access

  • Canned Sardines & Mackerel: Available at any local supermarket.
  • Atlantic Salmon: Found easily at local Halifax fish mongers.
  • Chia Seeds & Flaxseed: High ALA, found in standard baking aisles.
  • Walnuts: Standard bulk bin staple.

Omega-6 • Easy to Access

  • Sunflower & Canola Oil: Ubiquitous in standard grocery stores.
  • Peanut Butter: Standard pantry item everywhere.
  • Chicken Thighs: Highly accessible poultry staple.
  • Eggs & Tofu: Standard proteins found in any grocery layout.

Omega-3 • Hard to Access

  • High-Dose Algal Oil: Often requires visiting specialty supplement shops.
  • Wild-Caught Alaskan King Salmon: Expensive and seasonal in the East Coast.
  • Hemp Hearts: High ALA, but often restricted to specialty health food aisles.

Omega-6 • Hard to Access

  • Borage Oil: Specialty item primarily found in health food stores.
  • Evening Primrose Oil: Used for specific GLA supplementation.
  • Specialty Pine Nuts: Often out of stock or expensive.
  • Cold-pressed Grapeseed Oil: Harder to find pure, unrefined versions locally.
